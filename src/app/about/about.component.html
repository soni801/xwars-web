The Great X Wars

About

Overview

'The Great X Wars' is a slow-paced strategy game created by Soni and Magnus. The game plays out as crosses being placed on a grid, and the objective of the game is to fully invade the opponent's foundation before they invade yours.

Origin

The idea of the game sparked several years back when I had just started high school. Me and my classmate, Magnus, were very bored in class. At this point we had already played a lot of Tic-tac-toe together, as all students do. But that gets very repetitive over time, so Magnus found Strategic Tic-Tac-Toe for us to play. We really enjoyed it, but wanted to expand on the concept.

Originally, Magnus came with the idea to do the same, but recurse one more time. We tried, but it became very confusing. We then tried to make the game work using an entire page of the math book, you know, those with grids. When doing this, we naturally had to change the rules to fit this larger board. We very quickly decided to make the objective to reach the other side, and continued to implement new mechanics over time. We played this game for 3 years, always revising and changing the rules slightly to make the experience more fun. We dubbed it 'The Great X Wars'. Why? Don't ask me, it was Magnus' idea.

After 3 years of high school we chose different paths for the future, but remained close friends. Because we no longer met that often, Magnus came up with the idea for me to digitalise the game - he knew I had already been programming for several years. I accepted the challenge, and started immediately. The original code was horrible, and I very quickly stopped development on the project.

Several long pauses and many complete rewrites later, we're here. At a web-based client for the game. As for why I chose web? I originally tried making different types of native clients, but I saw that the industry as a whole was moving a lot towards web. In addition to this, a web based platform will always create better device support, e.g. mobile. So here it is, our 'classic' board game, but as a web application.

Rules

Objective

Destroy the enemy's foundation by invading it with your own tiles.

Before the first round

Each player has to choose a location for their foundation before the game starts. Your foundation must be touching your side of the board.

When it's your turn

You must do only one of the following:

  • Place a normal tile
    • You can only place a tile touching one of your own tiles. If you have no tiles, the tile must be touching your foundation unit.
  • Destroy an enemy tile
    • To destroy a single enemy tile you need to place two of your tiles on the enemy tile.
    • This has to be done over two turns, however these turns do not need to be back-to-back.
    • The enemy can never regain a destroyed tile.
  • Invade the enemy's foundation
    • If you have a tile next to the enemy's foundation unit, you can invade the foundation by placing one of your tiles inside the foundation unit. When the foundation unit is covered with your tiles, you win the game.
    • You may use one single large tile to win in one turn.

Large tiles

  • When you get three normal tiles in a line (either straight or diagonal), you get a large tile. A large tile's size is 2x2 tiles and must be placed immediately.
  • A tile may only be used once in each direction to make a large tile.
  • It is not possible to create large tiles using large tiles.

Misc.

  • If you make it impossible for the enemy to invade your foundation, you lose.
